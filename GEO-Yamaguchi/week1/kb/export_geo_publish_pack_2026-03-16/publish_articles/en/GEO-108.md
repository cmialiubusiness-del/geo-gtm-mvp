### TL;DR
- Outdated content can keep propagating in AI surfaces over time. [S05][S31]
- Content debt is usually a mix of stale facts, conflicting definitions, and broken evidence. [S06][S59]
- Versioned updates outperform blind mass deletion. [S61]

### Article
**Direct answer**: in AI surfaces, stale information is expensive because it can be repeatedly synthesized and re-exposed.

Governance steps:
1. Build a high-risk content inventory.
2. Add visible verification dates on critical pages.
3. Resolve conflicting definitions into one canonical version.
4. Add “current version notes” to legacy pages with ongoing traffic.

### FAQ
**Q1: Should all old pages be rewritten?** No.

**Q2: How do we prioritize debt-heavy pages?** Exposure, decision impact, and volatility.

**Q3: What counts as timely updates?** Topic-dependent; volatile topics often need weekly refresh.
