## Article GEO-201
### Page Metadata
- Slug: `/en/insights/geo-dual-engine-visibility-model`
- Meta Title: `GEO Is Not “New SEO”: A Dual-Engine Visibility Model for 2026`
- Meta Description: `A practical operating model for running SEO and GEO together in the AI search era.`

### TL;DR
- GEO does not replace SEO; it extends discoverability into citation-level visibility.[S01][S23][S24]
- Ranking still matters, but citation behavior does not fully mirror rankings.[S30][S60]
- Teams should run SEO and GEO as one integrated operating system.

### Background
In classic search, the primary objective was clicks. In AI answer environments, users often get a synthesized answer first and click second. That means being present in the answer itself is now a core visibility outcome.

The root causes of weak citation visibility are usually:
1. Technical gaps (crawl/index/canonical failures).
2. Content gaps (claims without clear evidence).
3. Operating gaps (traffic-only reporting, no citation monitoring).

### Dual-Engine Model
1. SEO engine: crawlability, indexability, discoverability, ranking opportunity.
2. GEO engine: interpretability, verifiability, citability, freshness.

These are not separate content systems. They are dual requirements for the same page: useful for readers and extractable for machines.

### 30-Day Action Plan
1. Week 1: technical baseline audit.
2. Week 2: rewrite top 20 high-intent pages with answer-first intros and evidence anchors.
3. Week 3: add FAQ/HowTo structures and normalize definitions.
4. Week 4: launch citation and narrative accuracy reporting.

## Article GEO-202
### Page Metadata
- Slug: `/en/insights/prompt-research-from-keywords-to-journeys`
- Meta Title: `Prompt Research in Practice: From Keywords to Question Journeys`
- Meta Description: `A practical workflow for collecting and publishing against real AI question journeys.`

### TL;DR
- Keyword research remains useful, but is no longer sufficient in AI contexts.[S46][S51]
- Prompt research should map first-question and follow-up-question chains.
- Keep a fixed update cadence, or coverage decays quickly.[S31][S61]

### Why Keyword-Only Strategy Underperforms
AI users ask longer, contextual, multi-step questions. If your content covers only the first question and not follow-ups, citation continuity breaks.

### 4-Step Workflow
1. Capture real prompts from support, sales, and internal query logs.
2. Cluster by intent: definition, comparison, how-to, risk, budget.
3. Map each cluster to existing pages and identify coverage gaps.
4. Rewrite titles, FAQ blocks, and step sections using real query language.

- Meta Title: `Defensive GEO: How to Manage Narrative Risk in AI Answers`
- Meta Description: `A governance model for reducing mis-citation, stale-fact spread, and narrative drift.`

### TL;DR
- The primary risk is often misinterpretation, not absence.[S15][S30]
- Defensive GEO requires claim consistency, evidence consistency, and update consistency.
- Compliance and access-rule shifts increase narrative risk exposure.[S50][S62]

### Core Risk Pattern
AI systems synthesize across sources. Conflicting definitions and stale claims can become amplified “consensus.”

### 3-Layer Governance
1. Claim governance: approved and prohibited wording.
2. Evidence governance: every major claim tied to source, time, and scope.
3. Distribution governance: website, FAQ, and case pages share one fact model.

## Article GEO-209
### Page Metadata
- Slug: `/en/insights/trust-signals-in-zero-click-era-longform`
- Meta Title: `Trust Strategy in the Zero-Click Era: Building Citable Authority Signals`
- Meta Description: `A longform framework for creating trust signals that improve AI citation probability.`

### TL;DR
- Zero-click does not eliminate content value; it shifts value toward trust and citation.[S25][S52]
- Authority is driven by relevance, consistency, and verifiability.[S28][S40]
- Owned and external signals must be designed as one system.[S39][S48]

### Why This Matters
The key question is no longer “Did we get the click first?” but “Were we trusted enough to be included in the answer?” If the answer layer excludes you, downstream conversion opportunities shrink.

### 3-Layer Trust Model
1. Owned trust signals: clear claims, transparent method, explicit limits.
2. Third-party trust signals: relevant, reviewable references.
3. Context consistency: aligned language across website and discussion surfaces.

### Practical Starter Actions
1. Convert core claims into evidence-backed statements.
2. Build standard correction answers for top 10 high-risk questions.
3. Maintain a monthly “expired evidence” cleanup list.
