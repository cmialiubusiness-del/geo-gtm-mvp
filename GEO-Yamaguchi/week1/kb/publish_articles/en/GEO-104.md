### TL;DR
- AI tends to cite pages with clear structure and complete evidence. [S16][S17]
- Citation performance is primarily a clarity problem, not a length problem. [S18][S59]
- FAQ blocks, how-to steps, and comparison tables are high-yield structures. [S07][S08]

### Article
**Direct answer**: citation consistency is engineered through structure, not luck.

10 actions:
1. Lead with a 60-90 word direct answer.
2. Keep one canonical definition per page.
3. Place evidence anchors directly after claims.
4. Attach dimensions to every comparison claim.
5. Add prerequisites and failure signals to step content.
6. Use real user wording in FAQ.
7. Add date and scope to key metrics.
8. Include non-applicable scenarios.
9. Standardize field labels across similar pages.
10. Display update timestamp and version notes.

### FAQ
**Q1: Are longer pages always better?** No.

**Q2: Is FAQ mandatory?** Strongly recommended.

**Q3: What is the minimum evidence anchor?** Date plus source ID.
