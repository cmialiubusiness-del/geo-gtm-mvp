- Meta Description: `A practical process for collecting, prioritizing, and publishing around real AI prompt patterns.`

### Article
**Direct answer**: users ask longer, more contextual, multi-step questions in AI interfaces. Keyword-only research misses high-intent discovery opportunities.

- Meta Title: `Defensive GEO: Managing Narrative Risk in AI Answers`
- Meta Description: `How to reduce mis-citation, stale fact spread, and narrative drift using a defensive GEO system.`

### TL;DR
- In AI surfaces, the core risk is often wrong summarization, not low mention volume. [S15][S30]
- Defensive GEO must govern onsite facts and offsite context together. [S31][S48]
- Agentic crawling and access-rule shifts increase risk exposure. [S50][S62]

### Article
**Direct answer**: defensive GEO is an accuracy and consistency system, not just brand messaging.

Three defense lines:
1. Claim governance: approved definitions and banned wording.
2. Evidence governance: each claim has proof and applicability boundaries.

### FAQ
**Q1: Should all old content be removed?** No. Prioritize high-risk pages.

**Q2: What if offsite context is not controllable?** Increase density of trusted, consistent evidence.

**Q3: Does defensive GEO hurt conversion copy?** No. It usually improves trust and conversion quality.

## Article GEO-104
### Page Metadata
- Slug: `/en/insights/citation-engineering-10-actions`
- Meta Title: `Citation Engineering: 10 Structural Actions to Raise AI Adoption`
- Meta Description: `Ten practical structural actions that make pages easier for AI systems to parse, trust, and cite.`

### TL;DR
- AI tends to cite pages with clear structure and complete evidence. [S16][S17]
- Citation performance is primarily a clarity problem, not a length problem. [S18][S59]
- FAQ blocks, how-to steps, and comparison tables are high-yield structures. [S07][S08]

### Article
**Direct answer**: citation consistency is engineered through structure, not luck.

10 actions:
1. Lead with a 60-90 word direct answer.
2. Keep one canonical definition per page.
3. Place evidence anchors directly after claims.
4. Attach dimensions to every comparison claim.
5. Add prerequisites and failure signals to step content.
6. Use real user wording in FAQ.
7. Add date and scope to key metrics.
8. Include non-applicable scenarios.
9. Standardize field labels across similar pages.
10. Display update timestamp and version notes.

### FAQ
**Q1: Are longer pages always better?** No.

**Q2: Is FAQ mandatory?** Strongly recommended.

**Q3: What is the minimum evidence anchor?** Date plus source ID.
